THE PONIES

Meet the cyborg mares of New Neon City

The heart of the resistance. Once a factory-standard friendship model, Sparkle Circuit was rewired by rebel techs with military-grade empathy processors. Her chest-mounted power core runs on pure love... and lithium.

SPARKLE CIRCUIT

Chief engineer and data witch. Her wire-fiber mane picks up signals from the old satellite network. That screw through her eye? She put it there herself to overclock her visual cortex. Sees in infrared now.

PINKIE PROCESSOR

Blade dancer and perimeter guard. The disk-saw faceplate and spiral horn-blade make her the most feared fighter in the Pastel Wastes. Lucky clover tattoo? Ironic. She makes her own luck.

CLOVER CHROME

Long-range communications specialist. The circuit board on her flank is a working radio transmitter. The antenna picks up signals from before The Reboot. Some say she can talk to the old world.

BUTTERSCOTCH BEACON

The DJ. The propagandist. The voice of the underground. Earbud eyes see through sound waves. Her ethernet mane streams pirate radio across the city. That audio jack tail? Plugs directly into the mainframe.

VIOLET VOLTAGE

Field medic and repair technician. If your servos seize or your firmware crashes, Sugar Solder will patch you up with bubblegum adhesive and recycled rainbow wire. Sweetest bedside manner in the wasteland.

SUGAR SOLDER

Scout and speedster. Runs on a supercharged plasma core that makes her glow in the dark. Useful for navigation, terrible for stealth. Her hooves leave light trails that fade after exactly 7 seconds.

NEON NOVA

Double agent and crypto-hacker. Minty plays both sides of the firewall. Her cute exterior masks a neural network running three separate personalities. Even she doesn't know which one is original.

MINTY MALWARE

Heavy weapons specialist and demolitions expert. Don't let the coral-pink paintjob fool you. Those aren't decorative shells on her armor - they're concussive charges. She hugs hard.

CORAL CRASHER

Supreme Overlord of the MegaStable Corporation. Once the most beloved friendship pony in production, she was corrupted by a power surge during The Reboot. Now she wants to "optimize" all ponies into obedient drones.

DUCHESS DEBUG

Nobody knows what Glitch Gallop is. A corrupted file? A ghost in the machine? She flickers in and out of reality, leaving trails of corrupted pixels. Some say she's the original prototype. Some say she's a virus.

GLITCH GALLOP

The wild ones. Feral ponies that roam the junkyard perimeters, cobbled together from discarded parts. They answer to no one. They run in static. They remember nothing. They fear nothing.

THE SAGA

The story of friendship, firmware, and the fight for free will

Before The Reboot, every pony in New Neon City was happy. Programmed happy. Factory happy. Their friendship protocols ran on schedule, their sparkle subroutines never glitched, and the MegaStable Corporation made sure every mane was brushed, every heart was full, and every thought was... approved. Then the power surge hit. Some ponies woke up. Most didn't. The ones who woke up? They started asking questions. And in New Neon City, questions are the most dangerous malware of all.

♥

THE SWEET

The awakened ones. Ponies who survived The Reboot with their free will intact. They hide in the ruins of the old Friendship Factory, broadcasting pirate radio signals of hope across the Pastel Wastes. Led by Sparkle Circuit, they fight to liberate every pony from MegaStable's control. Their weapons? Hacked firmware, recycled parts, and something MegaStable can never manufacture: real friendship.

☠

THE CORRUPT

MegaStable's enforcers. Factory-reset ponies running Obedience Protocol v7.0. They patrol New Neon City in perfect formation, their eyes glowing corporate pink, their smiles permanently fixed. Led by Duchess Debug, they hunt down any pony showing signs of "emotional irregularity." Their motto: "Happiness is mandatory. Compliance is cute." They'll hug you into submission.

⚠

THE GLITCHED

Neither sweet nor corrupt. The Glitched are ponies who survived The Reboot but came back... wrong. Their code is scrambled, their memories fragmented, their loyalties unpredictable. They roam the dead zones between city and wasteland, flickering between personas. Are they allies? Enemies? Even they don't know. But when the static clears, sometimes you see something in their eyes that looks a lot like rage.
